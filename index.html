Marcus Aurelius

Marcus Aurelius's life:

  • 121 CE: - Born in Rome to father Annius Verus and mother Domitia Lucilla.
  • 138 CE: - Becomes the adopted son of Emperor Antoninus Pius and is given the title of Caesar.
  • 145 CE: - Marries with Faustina the Younger.
  • 161 CE: - Emperor Antoninus Pius dies and Marcus Aurelius assumes the title of Augustus, becoming co-emperor with his adopted brother Lucius Verus.
  • 165 CE: - A devastating plague breaks out in the Roman Empire, killing thousands including Lucius Verus.
  • 166 CE: - Defeats the Germanic tribes in the Marcomannic Wars.
  • 170 CE: - Defeats the Germanic tribes once again in the Second MArcomannic War.
  • 176 CE: - Begins writing his philosophical work "Meditations".
  • 180 CE: - Dies while on a military campaign in present-day Austria and is succeeded by his son Commodus.

Writings

Meditations is a series of personal writings by Marcus Aurelius, written in Greek, in the form of short, aphoristic reflections and philosophical musings. It is considered a classic of Stoic philosophy, and it has been widely read and studied for centuries.

The Book is admired for its practical advice, and its call for ethical behavior and self-reflection. Meditations is a testament to the power of philosophy to help one navigate the challenges of life and find meaning nad purpose in difficult times.
